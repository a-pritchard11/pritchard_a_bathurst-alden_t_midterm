Is a Hot-Dog a Sandwich?

A Hot-Dog IS a Sandwich by Tegan Bathurst-Alden

I would argue that the traditional definition of a hot-dog fits the criteria of being a sandwich.
Many will argue about the standard that defines a sandwich, but most would agree that it is any food surrounded by bread that can be eaten
without getting said food on your hands. Considering this criteria, we can reasonably assume that a hot-dog does indeed qualify.

The first argument stems entirely from the insignificant fact that the meat of a hot-dog is a cylinder, but that is an irrelevant point.
When a ham lover rolls up their ham slices to make a sandwich, do the same rules apply? Would a hamburger, which is technically a very short cylinder,
also garner the same controversy? The basic structure in all of these remains the same; bread, subject, condiments, and bread.

The second argument would be that the bread in this case doesn't split entirely down the middle when a hot dog is considered.
This is another irrelevant point, as many sandwiches are made with bread that is still connected in some way between the two halves.
The company Subway, a company that prides itself entirely on sandwiches, doesn't cut their buns entirely in half.
All sub sandwiches would technically not qualify if this argument were valid in any way.

Lastly, people may argue the orientation of the hot-dog disregards it from being a sandwich. The fact that a typical sandwich is horizontal,
while a hot dog is more vertical, does not create a valid argument. Many people eat hot dogs horizontally, with the hot dog itself facing the side
and the buns surrounding it facing upwards and downwards respectfully, it all comes down to preference. I would argue that the preference in question,
the subjective handling methods of sandwiches and hot-dogs, is an irrelevant point.

In conclusion, a hot-dog does indeed meet the traditional criteria to be a sandwich, and many of the arguments against it are purely subjective in nature.
What cannot be ignored is the objective truth behind the matter that hot-dogs are sandwiches.
